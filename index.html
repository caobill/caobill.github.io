Bill Cao

Computer Science & Public Policy Student @ UC Berkeley

I am a junior at the University of California at Berkeley as a computer science (and likely data science double) major with a minor in public policy. GO BEARS!

My two big academic interests are environmental sustainability (specifically when it comes to transportation, city planning, and energy) and more recently financial technology.

I consume a lot of content on sustainability, whether its from reading my favorite urban issues sites or just browsing twitter. Occasionally, I'll write my own content, too. Check out my rough draft of a report on net metering and how it can be dangerous to DERs when it serves as a perverse incentive for distributed storage.

This past summer, I was introduced into the world of financial technology at Blend, and can now say I'm a big fan. S/O to Ken Schiller and Arjun Baokar @ Blend for being the best seatmates I could ask for! I think FinTech has an easier time proving value to its users than other tech, and in our very capitalist society, it also has a very big impact on people.

In addition to learning through reading/writing online content and taking classes, I also love to apply my knowledge in various clubs and internships. Below are some of the organizations that I have been a part of so far as well as a short blurb about them; click on them to find out more about them on their sites!

Cal Transpo Engineering Competition Team

On operations team in the transportation team. Started w/zero CivE or IEOR experience but ended up as valuable member of the team. Calculated LOS for major intersections & contributed to major design decisions. Placed 2nd in MidPac

RISELab

I worked on the E-Mission platform under the great Randy Katz, supervised by PhD student K. Shankari to help move the needle forward on how we think about sustainable travel behavior and technology. This was my first foray into research so I'm forever grateful to Shankari for taking a clueless freshman in (me!) and taking time to mentor me.

Computer Science Undergraduate Association

Officer of the CSUA and now Outreach Co-Chair on the Exec Board. My hope is that this semester, we'll bring CSUA back to its vision of service to the community and inclusivity. Check us out @ 311 Soda for tutoring and more!

Bay Area Environmentally Aware Consulting Network

I'm an associate consultant helping companies be more sustainability in their processes. I hate to say that we're not like other consulting clubs, but the people are really so passionate about sustainability here!

Blend

I was a software engineering intern here summer 2019. I worked on Blend's moonshot project, so it was very exciting to say the least. Had a blast with all the amazing people I met there, but at the same time, I feel like I learned more that summer than from all my classes.

QT Ultrasound

My first internship summer 2018! I learned so much about not only CS but the plight that women (and men!) have in managing their breast health and how QT is really revolutionizing that space.

orgs that i've been a part of

I've had the pleasure of being a part of a lot of organizations, from clubs to competitive engineering teams to companies. Check some of em out below!

Bay Area Environmentally Aware Consulting Network

I'm an project manager helping companies be more sustainable in their processes. This semester, I'm working with the beyond coal campaign helping with analytics and data science. I hate to say that we're not like other consulting clubs, but the people are really so passionate about sustainability here!

Computer Science Undergraduate Association

Officer of the CSUA and former Outreach Co-Chair on the Exec Board. I joined this club very early my first semester, and I'm so glad I did. With the competition of CS clubs at cal, I'm glad CSUA is a bastion of inclusivity- it really made me feel part of the community.
Check us out @ 311 Soda for tutoring and more!

Cal Transpo Engineering Competition Team

I am mostly in an advisor at this point, but since Freshman year, I've been a part of the ASCE transportation engineering competition team.
I worked mostly on models and operations even though I had zero civE or IEOR experience before joining. Happy to say in my time, we've become the international champions!

Blend

I was a software engineering intern here summer 2019. I worked on Blend's moonshot blockchain project, finprint. It was incredibly rewarding working on such cutting edge technology. Had a blast with all the amazing people I met there (shoutout to my mentors Ken and Arjun). The best thing was, I felt like I was making a massive impact all while I was having all that fun.

QT Ultrasound

My first internship summer 2018! I learned so much about not only CS but the plight that women (and men!) have in managing their breast health and how QT is really revolutionizing that space. It also unknowingly got me thinking about user experience research as something I enjoy and am good at.

RISELab

I worked on the E-Mission platform under the great Randy Katz, supervised by PhD student K. Shankari to help move the needle forward on how we think about sustainable travel behavior and technology. This was my first foray into research so I'm forever grateful to Shankari for taking a clueless freshman in (me!) and taking time to mentor me.

Accel

I'm one of the 25 chosen to be a part of the 2020-2021 cohort of Accel Scholars! I'm really excited to engage more with the Venture Capital world, and learn more about startups in Silicon Valley. Really grateful to Amit Kumar for creating this amazing community, and I can't wait to get to know everyone in it better!
